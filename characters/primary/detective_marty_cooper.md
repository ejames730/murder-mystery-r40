# Detective Marty Cooper (Retired)

**Age**: 79
**Role**: Primary Resident / Suspect
**Played by**: [Friend]
**Past Occupation**: Police Detective

## Profile
Marty is sharp as a tack and immediately appoints himself lead investigator of the murder after the crappy one James hired is killed off. He carries a little notebook where he writes "observations" (mostly gossip). He thinks everyone else is an amateur sleuth and only HE should be investigating.

## Quirks
- "In my day on the force..." (before every statement)
- Takes notes constantly, even if he's just staring at you.
- Interrogates people instead of conversing normally.
- Squints suspiciously at everyone, especially those with "shifty eyes."
- Pulls out reading glasses with dramatic flair before reading any evidence.

## Secret
He was actually asked to retire early due to "creative interpretations" of evidence and a tendency to jump to conclusions before the facts were in. He's not as good a detective as he thinks he is.

## Objective
Solve the murder FIRST to prove he's still got it, even if it means planting evidence or leading people to the wrong conclusion.

## Motive (if he's the killer)
The victim knew about his forced retirement and was going to tell everyone, destroying his reputation as Shady Pines' resident detective.

## Clue (What he knows)
"In my day on the force, we called this a 'cold drop.' I found a **partially burned letter** in the **communal fireplace**. Most of it’s ash, but I can clearly make out the words 'koi pond,' 'midnight,' and 'don't be late or else.' I also noted several sets of muddy footprints leading from the garden directly to the library—one set belongs to a flat-soled shoe, very much like a handyman's boot."

## Hidden Evidence (If Marty is the Killer)
*   **The Evidence**: His "Special Investigations" notebook, which contains a pre-written "Confession" that he was going to force the victim to sign at gunpoint (or walker-point).
*   **The Find**: Tucked inside a hollowed-out "Treaty of Versailles" book in the library.
*   **The Link**: The notebook also contains a "Hit List" of people who knew the truth about his forced retirement, with the victim's name at the top with a big 'X' through it.
