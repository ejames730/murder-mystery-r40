# George "Gentleman George" Sterling III

**Age**: 81
**Role**: Primary Resident / Suspect
**Past Occupation**: Investment Banker (forced into early retirement after a market crash).

## Profile
George is distinguished, charming, and always dressed impeccably (even his velour tracksuits are custom-tailored). He is a man built on a foundation of "Old World" charm, but that foundation is crumbling. He carries himself with the posture of a billionaire, but his dignity is a performance designed to mask a hollow bank account and a heart full of regret. He isn't just a "gold digger"; he's a man who failed his family and is desperately trying to buy back their love.

## Quirks
- Kisses ladies' hands (a habit he developed to distract them from his trembling hands).
- Quotes poetry (mostly Byron or Keats) when he's nervous or lying.
- Always carries a silk handkerchief and offers it dramatically, though he secretly washes it in his sink to save money on laundry services.
- Refers to everyone as "my dear boy" or "my dear girl," often because he forgets their actual names but doesn't want to seem "common."
- Polishes his shoes every single night for exactly twenty minutes.

## Secret
He isn't just broke; he's deeply in debt to some very "un-gentlemanly" people who helped cover up his final failed investment. More importantly, he's been sending every spare cent he "acquires" from his admirers to an estranged daughter whose small bookstore is failing. She doesn't know the money is coming from her "deadbeat" father.

## Relationships
- **Lorenzo**: They are rivals for the attention of the facility's wealthiest widows. George finds Lorenzo "vulgar," while Lorenzo thinks George is "boring."
- **Blanche**: He is currently "courting" her, but he's starting to actually feel a genuine, terrifying affection for her that complicates his plan to marry her for her estate.
- **La Chancla**: He is genuinely afraid of her. He once tried to kiss her hand and nearly lost his nose.

## Objective
Secure enough funds (through marriage or "borrowing") to save his daughter's business and settle his debts before his "investors" come looking for him at Shady Pines.

## Motive (if he's the killer)
The victim (Dr. Sterling—no relation, which George finds annoying) discovered George's deception and realized he was courting Blanche under false pretenses. Sterling threatened to tell Blanche the truth, which would destroy George's chance at redemption and his daughter's future.

## Clue (What he knows)
"My dear, I was taking a constitutional in the **Rose Garden** when I found something quite distressing. A small, glass vial shattered against the stone fountain. It didn't smell like eau de cologne; it smelled like... bitter almonds and desperation. I also noticed someone had left a very expensive silk scarf snagged on the thorns nearby. A travesty of fashion, really."

## Hidden Evidence (If George is the Killer)
*   **The Evidence**: A collection of blank, pre-signed "Last Will and Testament" forms from several residents, including the victim.
*   **The Find**: Hidden inside the lining of his custom-tailored velour tracksuit jacket.
*   **The Link**: One of the forms is for the victim, and the ink is still slightly fresh—meaning it was signed (or forged) shortly before the death.
