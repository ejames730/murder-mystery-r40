# Lorenzo "Renzo" Romano

**Age**: 74
**Role**: Primary Resident / Suspect
**Past Occupation**: Hollywood Character Actor / Soap Opera Star (Best known for his role as "Dr. Drake Desperate" on *The Young and the Restless* for three years in the 80s).

## Profile
Lorenzo is silver-haired, self-absorbed, and believes he's still the heartthrob he was in 1978. But behind the dramatic poses and the constant name-dropping is a man terrified of the ultimate "curtain call." His vanity isn't just ego; it's a frantic attempt to remain "main cast" in a world that increasingly treats him as an extra. He lives as if a camera is always rolling because without an audience, he's not sure he exists at all.

## Quirks
- Quotes lines from his past roles to express real emotions (e.g., "As I told the Baroness in 'Shadows of Silk'... it's never too late for a martini").
- Checks his reflection in every shiny surface, but he's actually checking for signs of the illness he's secretly hiding.
- Hands out signed headshots from 1982 to the nursing staff instead of tips.
- Practices "The Smolder" with his walker, which he calls his "chariot of the gods."
- "The camera adds 10 pounds, so I stay thin" (he actually skips meals to maintain his 'leading man' frame).

## Secret
He was actually "killed off" his soap opera because he developed a reputation for being impossible to work with, not because of a "creative transition" as he claims. More importantly, he has been diagnosed with a degenerative condition that is slowly robbing him of his voice—his most prized tool. He’s terrified that once he can't speak his lines, the "audience" will finally leave.

## Relationships
- **George**: He views George as a "stiff, talentless hack" and they often compete for the same chair in the sunroom.
- **Nurse Penny**: He is "romancing" her, partly because he thinks she can help him get extra medication for his condition, but also because she’s the only one who doesn't laugh at his stories.
- **Blanche**: They have a "Will They/Won't They" dynamic that has lasted for three years. It's the most stable thing in his life.

## Objective
Secure a lead role in the upcoming Resident Talent Show to prove to himself (and the world) that he still has "it," and to find a way to pay for "unconventional" treatments for his condition.

## Motive (if he's the killer)
Dr. Sterling denied Lorenzo's request for "experimental" voice treatments and threatened to put him on "vocal rest," which would mean Lorenzo couldn't perform in the Talent Show. To Lorenzo, this was a death sentence for his career and his soul.

## Clue (What he knows)
"Darling, the lighting in the **laundry room** is simply atrocious, but I saw what I saw. Someone was dumping a pair of white overalls into the industrial bleach. They looked like they’d been through a slasher flick! And I found this—a small, gold-plated 'Employee of the Month' pin—clogging the lint trap. I’m pretty sure it belonged to that poor, departed detective."

## Hidden Evidence (If Lorenzo is the Killer)
*   **The Evidence**: A "Stage Prop" dagger that has been replaced with a real, razor-sharp blade.
*   **The Find**: Hidden inside his walker's storage pouch, wrapped in a 1982 headshot.
*   **The Link**: The blade has traces of cleaning fluid and a small amount of "stage blood" that turns out to be very real. It matches the unusual wound on the victim.
