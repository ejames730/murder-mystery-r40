# Gerald "The Spark" Miller - Activities Director

**Age**: 38
**Role**: NPC / Staff Member
**Occupation**: Fitness & Wellness Coordinator at Shady Pines (Self-styled "Senior Games Coach").

## Profile
Gerald is relentlessly upbeat, aggressively energetic, and completely oblivious to the fact that his "target demographic" mostly just wants to nap. He views Shady Pines as a high-performance training facility and himself as an elite coach. However, Gerald’s "toxic positivity" is a frantic mask. He is a man who suffered a massive nervous breakdown while working as a high-stakes corporate recruiter and has dedicated himself to "fixing" others to avoid fixing himself. Every whistle-blow is a heartbeat of his own anxiety being kept at bay.

## Quirks
- "That's totally rad, my dudes! Who's ready for some BURPEE BINGO?!"
- Carries a silver whistle around his neck and blows it to "reset the vibe" when things get too quiet or serious.
- "Hydrate or die-drate, friends! Take a sip!" (He drinks 4 liters of neon-green energy drinks a day).
- Wears an aggressively clean neon headband and a moisture-wicking polo.
- Interrupts serious conversations for "30 seconds of jazz hands" to increase heart rates; he genuinely believes movement cures sadness.
- "Don't be a square! Get groovy with it! High knees, Sophia! High knees!"

## Secret
Gerald is actually living in his car in the facility's back parking lot because he lost his house in a divorce that he refuses to acknowledge is real. He keeps his "life" in a series of neatly labeled bins in the equipment shed. He also has a secret obsession with "Old Hollywood" and spends his nights watching old films on a portable DVD player, dreaming of a life with more "substance."

## Relationships
- **Lorenzo**: Gerald idolizes Lorenzo because he represents the "Old Hollywood" glamour Gerald loves. He often asks Lorenzo for "acting tips" (which Lorenzo loves).
- **Sophia**: She is his "White Whale." He is determined to make her smile or participate in one activity. Her constant rejection fuels his manic energy.
- **Dr. Sterling**: Gerald is terrified of the Director. He knows his "performance" is the only thing keeping him employed and off the streets.

## What He Knows (Clue Distribution)
"Bro, it was totally weird! I was doing my 11 PM 'Sleep-Check Sprint' through the **north wing** and I saw someone in a hospital gown—except they were wearing heavy combat boots. Talk about a fashion faux pas! They were dragging a heavy laundry bag toward the **elevator**. It had a very... 'body-shaped' lumpy quality to it. I thought it was just a lot of towels!"

## Hidden Evidence (If Gerald is the Killer)
*   **The Evidence**: A "Workout Log" that doesn't track exercise, but rather the exact "Response Times" of the night shift nurses and the victim's heart medication schedule.
*   **The Find**: Tucked under the driver's seat of his car in the parking lot.
*   **The Link**: The log includes a detailed plan to "eliminate the distraction" (the victim) to maintain the "purity of the training environment" (Shady Pines).


## Character Trait
Desperately seeking validation. Hiding a crumbling personal life behind a "Fitbit-approved" smile.

## How to Interact
He'll share his clues if you:
- Agree to attend one of his activities (even just for 5 minutes).
- "High-five" him with enough enthusiasm to satisfy his need for connection.
- Ask about his "Coach's Handbook" (his clipboard).
- Tell him he's "doing a great job"—it will literally bring a tear to his eye and he'll spill everything.
