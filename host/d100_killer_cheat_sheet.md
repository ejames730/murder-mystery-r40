## The Killer Profiles

### **01-06: Arthur Montgomery (The General)**
*   **Motive**: Dr. Sterling threatened to kick him out for "disruptive behavior" (inspections). Arthur viewed him as a corrupt officer who had to be "relieved of duty" for the unit's safety.
*   **The Evidence**: A handwritten "After Action Report" detailing the target's elimination.
*   **The Find**: False bottom of his army footlocker.
*   **The Link**: Written in Arthur's tactical terminology on the back of a Shady Pines menu.

### **07-12: George Sterling (The Gentleman)**
*   **Motive**: Dr. Sterling discovered George was courtship-scamming wealthy widows and threatened to tell Blanche, which would end George's chance to save his daughter's bookstore.
*   **The Evidence**: Pre-signed "Last Will and Testament" forms, including the victim's.
*   **The Find**: Inside the lining of his velour tracksuit jacket.
*   **The Link**: The ink on the victim's form is fresh, suggesting it was forced or forged tonight.

### **13-18: Lorenzo Romano (The Lover)**
*   **Motive**: Dr. Sterling denied Lorenzo experimental voice treatments and threatened to put him on "vocal rest," preventing him from performing in the Talent Show—his last "curtain call."
*   **The Evidence**: A "Stage Prop" dagger replaced with a real blade.
*   **The Find**: Hidden in his walker's pouch, wrapped in an old headshot.
*   **The Link**: Blade has cleaning fluid and real blood matching the victim's unique wound.

### **19-24: Maria Garcia (La Chancla)**
*   **Motive**: The victim refused to pay a $500 gambling debt and insulted Maria by calling her "just a little old lady."
*   **The Evidence**: A lead-core weighted rubber slipper.
*   **The Find**: "Jai Alai Equipment" box under her bed.
*   **The Link**: Matches the circular indentation on the victim's temple and has traces of their hair pomade.

### **25-30: Dorothy Morrison (The Widow)**
*   **Motive**: The victim discovered she was dating two men simultaneously and threatened to reveal it, ruining her "devoted widow" reputation.
*   **The Evidence**: A blood-stained mourning veil and a "Dear Harold" confession letter.
*   **The Find**: Behind a photo on her "Harold Tribute Shelf."
*   **The Link**: The veil has a tear that matches fabric found in the victim's hand.

### **31-36: Rose Henderson (The Gossip)**
*   **Motive**: The victim was going to shut down her underground gambling ring and report her to the board.
*   **The Evidence**: A "Ledger of Debts" with the entry: 'DEATH SETTLES ALL ACCOUNTS.'
*   **The Find**: Empty Metamucil container in her bathroom.
*   **The Link**: Written in her distinctive "LOUD" handwriting.

### **37-42: Blanche Devereaux (The Flirt)**
*   **Motive**: The victim discovered she was an undercover administrator's daughter investigating fraud and threatened to "blow her cover."
*   **The Evidence**: A digital camera/recorder hidden inside a brooch.
*   **The Find**: Her jewelry box, under a pile of scarves.
*   **The Link**: Recording of the murder and a fake blackmail note she used to lure the victim.

### **43-48: Sophia Petrillo (The Curmudgeon)**
*   **Motive**: The victim was pressuring her to leave her fortune to them and became aggressive.
*   **The Evidence**: Industrial piano wire and a map of security camera blind spots.
*   **The Find**: Under her seat in the Library Annex.
*   **The Link**: The wire matches the ligature marks on the victim's neck.

### **49-54: Marge Cooper (The Detective)**
*   **Motive**: The victim knew Marge was forced into retirement for "evidence tampering" and was going to expose her "amateur" status to the residents.
*   **The Evidence**: A "Special Investigations" notebook with a pre-written confession and a Hit List.
*   **The Find**: Hollowed-out book (*Treaty of Versailles*) in the library.
*   **The Link**: Victim's name is at the top of the list with a big 'X' through it.

### **55-60: Penny Pincher (The Nurse)**
*   **Motive**: Denied her first weekend off in two years and threatened with a performance review after she complained about supply shortages.
*   **The Evidence**: A tampered "Code Blue" kit with a paralyzing agent.
*   **The Find**: Personal locker, in a double-bottomed medical bag.
*   **The Link**: Checkout timestamp of 0200 hours contradicts her alibi.

### **61-66: Birthday Girl (The New Resident)**
*   **Motive**: (User's choice, e.g., she's actually a relative of someone the victim wronged, or she just snapped at being called "naive.")
*   **The Evidence**: Victim's wallet and frosting-stained gloves.
*   **The Find**: Trunk of her car or under her booth.
*   **The Link**: Frosting matches the grab marks on the victim's face.

### **67-72: James Tasker (The Director)**
*   **Motive**: Slippery Sam was blackmailing him over "creative accounting" used to keep the facility afloat.
*   **The Evidence**: Empty Social Fund ledger and a blackmail payment receipt.
*   **The Find**: "Budget Savings" safe in his office (Code 0000).
*   **The Link**: Payment matches the blackmail demand found on the victim.

### **73-78: Frank Morrison (The Handyman)**
*   **Motive**: (Former journalist) The victim discovered Frank's secret files on the residents and was going to "sell the story" himself.
*   **The Evidence**: A Master Key and a folder of compromising surveillance photos.
*   **The Find**: Hollowed-out "Home Repair" manual in the maintenance closet.
*   **The Link**: Photos taken from angles only accessible via maintenance shafts.

### **79-84: Gerald Miller (The Spark)**
*   **Motive**: The victim discovered Gerald was living in his car and threatened to have him fired for "violating facility residency codes."
*   **The Evidence**: A Workout Log tracking nurse response times.
*   **The Find**: Under the driver's seat of his car.
*   **The Link**: Includes a plan to "eliminate the distraction" (the victim).

### **85-90: Diane (Front Desk)**
*   **Motive**: Stealing social security checks from residents; the victim caught her "redirecting" their mail.
*   **The Evidence**: Stolen checks and a blood-stained forged Power of Attorney.
*   **The Find**: False bottom in her desk drawer.
*   **The Link**: The checks have the victim's blood on the corner.

### **91-95: Nurse Margaret (The NPC Nurse)**
*   **Motive**: She felt the victim was "suffering" too much (or was too annoying) and decided to "help" them move on.
*   **The Evidence**: A Nurse's Log of 'Mercy Killings.'
*   **The Find**: Box of oversized diapers in the supply closet.
*   **The Link**: Victim is marked with a 'Special Care' star at the exact time of death.

### **96-00: Beatrice Sullivan (The Chef)**
*   **Motive**: Banned from the kitchen and threatened with transfer to a "lukewarm oatmeal" facility.
*   **The Evidence**: Concentrated extract of Bitter Almonds (Cyanide).
*   **The Find**: Behind "Generic Flour" in the pantry.
*   **The Link**: Smudge of Himalayan Pink Salt (her signature) on the lid.
